## Section Overview

Before we can talk about vector spaces, we need to know what scalars *are*.
The answer: scalars come from a **field** — an algebraic structure with addition and multiplication satisfying a set of axioms.
And fields are built on an even more primitive notion: a **group**.

This section establishes the algebraic foundation that underpins everything in the course.

### Why start here?

In most linear algebra courses, scalars are just real numbers, taken for granted.
MA2034 works over **any field** — including $\mathbb{C}$, finite fields $\mathbb{F}_p$, and more.
Understanding what makes $\mathbb{R}$ and $\mathbb{C}$ special (vs. $\mathbb{Z}$, which is *not* a field) matters the moment we ask: "does every system of equations have a solution?"

### Connection to what follows

Every vector space is defined over a **field** $F$. The scalars used to scale vectors must form a field so that equations like $\alpha(\mathbf{u} + \mathbf{v}) = \alpha\mathbf{u} + \alpha\mathbf{v}$ are meaningful and invertible.
