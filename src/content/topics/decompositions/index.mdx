## Section Overview

Factorizing a matrix into simpler pieces is one of the most powerful techniques in all of mathematics.
Each decomposition reveals a different aspect of the underlying linear transformation:

| Decomposition | What it reveals |
|---|---|
| **Spectral Theorem** | Normal operators diagonalize in an orthonormal basis |
| **Cholesky** | Symmetric positive definite = "square root" |
| **Jordan Normal Form** | Structure of non-diagonalizable operators |
| **QR** | Orthonormal basis from any basis; least-squares |
| **SVD** | Geometry of *any* map: stretch along principal axes |

This section is the capstone of the course — every decomposition builds on material from all previous sections.

### The Five Key Ideas

1. **Hermitian & Unitary Operators** — spectral theorem: normal operators are orthogonally diagonalizable
2. **Positive Definite Matrices** — inner product in disguise; Cholesky factorization $A = LL^*$
3. **Jordan Normal Form** — the finest possible block-diagonal form for any operator over $\mathbb{C}$
4. **QR Decomposition** — Gram–Schmidt as matrix factorization $A = QR$, least squares
5. **Singular Value Decomposition** — $A = U\Sigma V^*$; the universal factorization

### Definitions in this section
- **Def 28** — Hermitian (self-adjoint) operator; Hermitian matrix ($A = A^*$)
- **Def 29** — Unitary operator; unitary matrix ($U^*U = I$)
- **Def 30** — Positive definite, positive semidefinite, negative definite matrices
- **Def 31** — Cholesky factorization
- **Def 32** — Jordan block $J_k(\lambda)$; Jordan normal form
- **Def 33** — Singular values $\sigma_i$; SVD

### Theorems in this section
- **Thm 34 (Spectral Theorem)** — Every Hermitian operator on a finite-dimensional inner product space is unitarily diagonalizable
- **Thm 35** — Characterizations of positive definiteness (all principal minors, Cholesky, eigenvalues)
- **Thm 36 (Jordan Theorem)** — Every matrix over $\mathbb{C}$ has a Jordan normal form, unique up to permutation of blocks
- **Thm 37 (QR)** — Every $m \times n$ matrix with linearly independent columns has a QR factorization
- **Thm 38 (SVD)** — Every $m \times n$ matrix has an SVD $A = U\Sigma V^*$ with $\sigma_1 \geq \sigma_2 \geq \cdots \geq 0$
