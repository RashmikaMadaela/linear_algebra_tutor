## Big Idea

Every linear map $T: V \to W$ has two canonically associated subspaces: the **kernel** (what maps to zero) and the **image** (what gets hit). Their dimensions are linked by one of the most powerful results in linear algebra: the **Rank-Nullity Theorem**.

---

## Kernel and Image

<DefinitionBox number={17} title="Kernel and Image">
Let $T: V \to W$ be a linear map.

- The **kernel** (or **null space**) of $T$ is:
$$\ker(T) = \{\mathbf{v} \in V \mid T(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{0}_W\}$$

- The **image** (or **range**) of $T$ is:
$$\operatorname{Im}(T) = \{T(\mathbf{v}) \mid \mathbf{v} \in V\} = T(V)$$

- The **nullity** of $T$: $\operatorname{nullity}(T) = \dim \ker(T)$
- The **rank** of $T$: $\operatorname{rank}(T) = \dim \operatorname{Im}(T)$
</DefinitionBox>

---

## Kernel and Image are Subspaces

<TheoremBox number={19} title="Ker(T) ≤ V and Im(T) ≤ W" proofSketch={<>Ker: T(0)=0 so 0∈ker(T). If T(u)=T(v)=0 then T(αu+βv)=αT(u)+βT(v)=0, so ker(T) is closed under linear combinations. Im: T(0)=0 so 0∈Im(T). T(αu+βv)=αT(u)+βT(v) so Im(T) is closed under linear combinations.</>}>
For any linear $T: V \to W$:

1. $\ker(T)$ is a **subspace** of $V$.
2. $\operatorname{Im}(T)$ is a **subspace** of $W$.
</TheoremBox>

---

## Injectivity and Surjectivity

<TheoremBox number={20} title="Injectivity ↔ Trivial Kernel" proofSketch={<>(⇒) If T is injective and T(v)=0=T(0), then v=0. (⇐) If ker(T)={0} and T(u)=T(v), then T(u-v)=0, so u-v∈ker(T)={0}, thus u=v.</>}>
Let $T: V \to W$ be linear. Then:
- $T$ is **injective** (one-to-one) $\iff$ $\ker(T) = \{\mathbf{0}\}$
- $T$ is **surjective** (onto) $\iff$ $\operatorname{Im}(T) = W$
</TheoremBox>

## The Rank-Nullity Theorem

<TheoremBox number={21} title="Rank-Nullity Theorem (Dimension Theorem)" proofSketch={<>Let k₁,…,kₚ be a basis for ker(T) (nullity=p). Extend to a basis k₁,…,kₚ,v₁,…,vᵣ for V (dim V=p+r). Claim: T(v₁),…,T(vᵣ) is a basis for Im(T). Spanning: for any T(v)∈Im(T), write v=Σαᵢkᵢ+Σβⱼvⱼ, so T(v)=ΣβⱼT(vⱼ). LI: if ΣβⱼT(vⱼ)=0 then Σβⱼvⱼ∈ker(T), so all βⱼ=0 by linear independence of the extended basis. Hence rank(T)=r and nullity+rank=p+r=dim V. ∎</>}>
Let $V$ be finite-dimensional and $T: V \to W$ linear. Then:

$$\dim \ker(T) + \dim \operatorname{Im}(T) = \dim V$$
$$\operatorname{nullity}(T) + \operatorname{rank}(T) = \dim V$$

**Consequences:**
- $\operatorname{rank}(T) \leq \min(\dim V, \dim W)$
- If $\dim V = \dim W$: $T$ injective $\iff$ $T$ surjective $\iff$ $T$ bijective
</TheoremBox>

## Solving $T(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{w}$

<TheoremBox number={22} title="Solution Structure" proofSketch={<>If T(v₀)=w, then T(v)=w iff T(v-v₀)=0 iff v-v₀∈ker(T) iff v∈v₀+ker(T). This is exactly the coset structure from group theory applied to (V,+).</>}>
Let $T: V \to W$ be linear and $\mathbf{w} \in W$.

- If $T(\mathbf{v}_0) = \mathbf{w}$ (a **particular solution** exists), then the complete solution set is:
$$\{\mathbf{v} \in V \mid T(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{w}\} = \mathbf{v}_0 + \ker(T)$$

- No solution exists iff $\mathbf{w} \notin \operatorname{Im}(T)$.

This is the abstract analogue of: *general solution = particular solution + homogeneous solution* for $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$.
</TheoremBox>

<SummaryBox title="Key Takeaways: Kernel and Image">
- $\ker(T) \leq V$ and $\operatorname{Im}(T) \leq W$ are always subspaces.
- $T$ is injective $\iff$ $\ker(T) = \{\mathbf{0}\}$.
- $T$ is surjective $\iff$ $\operatorname{Im}(T) = W$.
- **Rank-Nullity:** $\operatorname{nullity}(T) + \operatorname{rank}(T) = \dim V$.
- For equal-dim spaces: injective $\iff$ surjective $\iff$ bijective.
- Solution set of $T(\mathbf{v}) = \mathbf{w}$ is $\mathbf{v}_0 + \ker(T)$ (coset of the kernel).
</SummaryBox>
