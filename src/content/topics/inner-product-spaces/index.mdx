## Section Overview

Vector spaces give us *algebraic* structure: addition and scalar multiplication.
This section adds **geometric** structure by introducing the **inner product** — a way to measure lengths and angles in any vector space.

From the inner product we build up a rich theory:

1. **Inner products** give a notion of length (norm) and angle (orthogonality).
2. **Cauchy-Schwarz and Triangle inequalities** are the two fundamental bounds.
3. **Orthonormal sets** are linearly independent by Theorem 20 — the ideal kind of basis.
4. **Gram-Schmidt** converts any LI set into an orthonormal one — the algorithmic heart of the section.
5. **Orthogonal polynomials** (Legendre, Laguerre, Chebyshev) are the Gram-Schmidt output for monomial sequences with classical weight functions.
6. **Orthogonal complements** and the **best approximation theorem** extend projection to abstract spaces — the theoretical foundation for least squares.
7. **Norms and completeness** lead to Banach spaces (complete normed spaces) and Hilbert spaces (complete inner product spaces).
8. **Metric spaces** are the most general setting — every normed space is a metric space, but not vice versa.
