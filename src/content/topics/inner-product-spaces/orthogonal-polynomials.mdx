## Big Idea

Applying Gram-Schmidt to the monomial sequence $\{1, x, x^2, x^3, \ldots\}$ with different inner products (different weight functions or integration domains) produces three classical families of orthogonal polynomials:
**Legendre**, **Laguerre**, and **Chebyshev** polynomials.
Each family is the natural orthonormal basis for a particular function space, and each arises as the solution of a specific second-order ODE.
These polynomials appear throughout applied mathematics, physics, and numerical analysis.

## Note 6 — Three Classical Families

### Legendre Polynomials

**Inner product:** $\langle f,g\rangle = \int_{-1}^{1} f(x)g(x)\,dx$ on $C[-1,1]$.

**First few polynomials** (unnormalized, then Gram-Schmidt normalized):
<BM math="P_0(x) = 1,\quad P_1(x) = x,\quad P_2(x) = \frac{1}{2}(3x^2-1),\quad P_3(x) = \frac{1}{2}(5x^3-3x)" />

**Orthogonality:** $\langle P_m, P_n\rangle = \dfrac{2}{2n+1}\delta_{mn}$

**Normalization:** $\tilde{P}_n = \sqrt{\dfrac{2n+1}{2}}\,P_n$ gives $\|\tilde{P}_n\|=1$.

**Generating ODE (Legendre's equation):**
<BM math="(1-x^2)y'' - 2xy' + n(n+1)y = 0, \quad n = 0,1,2,\ldots" />
The polynomial $P_n(x)$ is the solution of this ODE that is bounded on $[-1,1]$.

**Recurrence relation:**
<BM math="(n+1)P_{n+1}(x) = (2n+1)xP_n(x) - nP_{n-1}(x)" />

**Applications:** Solving Laplace's equation in spherical coordinates; approximating functions on $[-1,1]$; Gauss-Legendre quadrature.

---

### Laguerre Polynomials

**Inner product:** $\langle f,g\rangle = \int_0^{\infty} f(x)g(x)\,e^{-x}\,dx$ on $[0,\infty)$ (weighted $L^2$).

**First few polynomials:**
<BM math="L_0(x) = 1,\quad L_1(x) = 1-x,\quad L_2(x) = 1 - 2x + \tfrac{x^2}{2},\quad L_3(x) = 1 - 3x + \tfrac{3x^2}{2} - \tfrac{x^3}{6}" />

**Orthogonality:** $\langle L_m, L_n\rangle = \delta_{mn}$ (already orthonormal under the weighted inner product).

**Generating ODE (Laguerre's equation):**
<BM math="xy'' + (1-x)y' + ny = 0, \quad n = 0,1,2,\ldots" />

**Recurrence relation:**
<BM math="(n+1)L_{n+1}(x) = (2n+1-x)L_n(x) - nL_{n-1}(x)" />

**Applications:** Quantum mechanics (hydrogen atom radial wave functions, where the weight $e^{-x}$ arises naturally); probability (exponential family distributions).

---

### Chebyshev Polynomials

**Inner product:** $\langle f,g\rangle = \int_{-1}^{1} \dfrac{f(x)g(x)}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\,dx$ on $[-1,1]$ (weighted with $w(x) = (1-x^2)^{-1/2}$).

**Definition via trigonometry:**
<BM math="T_n(\cos\theta) = \cos(n\theta), \quad n = 0,1,2,\ldots" />

This is the defining property: $T_n$ evaluated at $\cos\theta$ gives $\cos(n\theta)$.

**First few polynomials** (obtained by expanding $\cos(n\theta)$ via de Moivre):
<BM math="T_0(x) = 1,\quad T_1(x) = x,\quad T_2(x) = 2x^2-1,\quad T_3(x) = 4x^3-3x" />

**Orthogonality:**
<BM math="\int_{-1}^1 \frac{T_m(x)T_n(x)}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\,dx = \begin{cases} 0 & m\neq n \\ \pi & m=n=0 \\ \pi/2 & m=n\neq 0 \end{cases}" />

**Generating ODE (Chebyshev's equation):**
<BM math="(1-x^2)y'' - xy' + n^2 y = 0" />

**Recurrence relation:**
<BM math="T_{n+1}(x) = 2x\,T_n(x) - T_{n-1}(x)" />

**Extremal property:** Among all monic polynomials of degree $n$, $2^{1-n}T_n(x)$ has the smallest maximum absolute value on $[-1,1]$. This makes Chebyshev polynomials ideal for minimax polynomial approximation.

**Applications:** Polynomial approximation with controlled error; Chebyshev spectral methods for PDEs; fast transforms.

## Common Mistakes

⚠ **Different inner products:** Each polynomial family is orthogonal with respect to a **specific** inner product / weight function. Legendre polynomials are **not** orthogonal under the Laguerre weight — always specify the inner product.

⚠ **Normalization:** Legendre polynomials $P_n$ satisfy $P_n(1)=1$ (standard normalization) but are **not** unit vectors — $\|P_n\|^2 = 2/(2n+1)$. The factors like $\sqrt{(2n+1)/2}$ are often omitted in tables.

⚠ **Domain:** Legendre lives on $[-1,1]$, Laguerre on $[0,\infty)$, Chebyshev on $[-1,1]$ — using the wrong domain breaks orthogonality.

## Summary

<SummaryBox title="Special Orthogonal Polynomials — Note 6">

| Family | Domain | Weight $w(x)$ | ODE |
|--------|--------|--------------|-----|
| Legendre $P_n$ | $[-1,1]$ | $1$ | $(1-x^2)y''-2xy'+n(n+1)y=0$ |
| Laguerre $L_n$ | $[0,\infty)$ | $e^{-x}$ | $xy''+(1-x)y'+ny=0$ |
| Chebyshev $T_n$ | $[-1,1]$ | $(1-x^2)^{-1/2}$ | $(1-x^2)y''-xy'+n^2y=0$ |

**Chebyshev key identity:** $T_n(\cos\theta) = \cos(n\theta)$.

All three arise from Gram-Schmidt applied to $\{1,x,x^2,\ldots\}$ with the respective weighted inner product.
</SummaryBox>
