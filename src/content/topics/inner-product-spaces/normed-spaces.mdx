## Big Idea

A norm is a length function.
Completeness upgrades a normed space to a Banach space.
If the norm comes from an inner product and the space is complete, we get a Hilbert space.

This is the key structural ladder used in modern linear analysis.

## Intuition

Norm tells you the size of one vector.
Metric tells you distance between two vectors.
Completeness tells you limit behavior is stable inside the same space.

Without completeness, Cauchy sequences can approach objects that are not in the space.

## Norm Definition

<DefinitionBox number={17} title="Norm">
A norm on a vector space $V$ is a function $\|\cdot\| : V \to \mathbb{R}$ such that for all vectors $u,v$ and scalar $a$:

1. $\|v\| \ge 0$ and $\|v\|=0$ iff $v=0$
2. $\|av\|=|a|\,\|v\|$
3. $\|u+v\| \le \|u\|+\|v\|$
</DefinitionBox>

## Cauchy And Completeness

A sequence $(v_n)$ is Cauchy if distances between later terms become arbitrarily small.
A normed space is complete if every Cauchy sequence converges in the same space.

More explicitly, $(v_n)$ is Cauchy when for every $\varepsilon>0$ there exists $N$ such that

<BM math="\|v_m-v_n\|<\varepsilon \text{ whenever } m,n>N" />

Convergent sequences are always Cauchy.
The converse is true exactly in complete spaces.

## Banach And Hilbert

- Banach space: complete normed space.
- Hilbert space: complete inner product space.
- Every Hilbert space is Banach.

And every Banach space is normed, every normed space is metric.

## Parallelogram Test

A norm comes from an inner product exactly when the parallelogram identity holds:

<BM math="\|u+v\|^2 + \|u-v\|^2 = 2\|u\|^2 + 2\|v\|^2" />

This is a practical test to separate Hilbert-type geometry from general Banach geometry.

## Standard Examples

- $\mathbb{R}^n$ with Euclidean norm: Hilbert.
- $C[a,b]$ with sup norm: Banach.
- $L^2[a,b]$: Hilbert.

Additional perspective:

- $\ell^1$ is Banach but not Hilbert.
- $\ell^2$ is Hilbert.
- Finite-dimensional normed spaces are always Banach.

## Why This Matters In This Course

- Gram-Schmidt and orthogonal projection live naturally in Hilbert settings.
- Least squares is geometric in inner product spaces.
- Numerical algorithms use norm-based error control and convergence statements.

## Common Mistakes

- Thinking Banach means finite-dimensional.
- Confusing Cauchy with convergent in non-complete spaces.
- Assuming every norm is induced by an inner product.

## Summary

<SummaryBox title="Normed, Banach, Hilbert">
- Norm gives length.
- Completeness gives Banach.
- Inner product plus completeness gives Hilbert.
- Hierarchy: Hilbert subset Banach subset Normed subset Metric.
- Parallelogram identity detects inner-product norms.
</SummaryBox>
