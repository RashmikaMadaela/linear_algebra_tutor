## Big Idea

A norm is a length function.
Completeness upgrades a normed space to a Banach space.
If the norm comes from an inner product and the space is complete, we get a Hilbert space.

## Norm Definition

<DefinitionBox number={17} title="Norm">
A norm on a vector space $V$ is a function $\|\cdot\| : V \to \mathbb{R}$ such that for all vectors $u,v$ and scalar $a$:

1. $\|v\| \ge 0$ and $\|v\|=0$ iff $v=0$
2. $\|av\|=|a|\,\|v\|$
3. $\|u+v\| \le \|u\|+\|v\|$
</DefinitionBox>

## Cauchy And Completeness

A sequence $(v_n)$ is Cauchy if distances between later terms become arbitrarily small.
A normed space is complete if every Cauchy sequence converges in the same space.

## Banach And Hilbert

- Banach space: complete normed space.
- Hilbert space: complete inner product space.
- Every Hilbert space is Banach.

## Standard Examples

- $\mathbb{R}^n$ with Euclidean norm: Hilbert.
- $C[a,b]$ with sup norm: Banach.
- $L^2[a,b]$: Hilbert.

## Summary

<SummaryBox title="Normed, Banach, Hilbert">
- Norm gives length.
- Completeness gives Banach.
- Inner product plus completeness gives Hilbert.
- Hierarchy: Hilbert subset Banach subset Normed subset Metric.
</SummaryBox>
